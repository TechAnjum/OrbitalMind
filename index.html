Mission Overview

Why Space Debris
Is a Critical Threat

The Sun-Synchronous Orbit band (800–1000 km) hosts ISRO's most vital Earth observation satellites — and it's increasingly crowded with debris.

🛰️

Kessler Syndrome Risk

A cascading collision chain could render entire orbital shells unusable for decades, cutting off crucial Earth observation and communication infrastructure.

📡

ISRO IRS Satellite Zone

India's Remote Sensing satellites — IRS-1A through Cartosat series — operate precisely in the 800–1000km SSO band, making this analysis directly actionable.

💥

Fragmentation Events

Single collision events generate thousands of untracked fragments. The 2007 Fengyun-1C ASAT test alone produced 3,000+ trackable objects.

🤖

ML-Powered Detection

DBSCAN excels at finding debris "clouds" of arbitrary shape without assuming cluster count — ideal for the chaotic, non-spherical distributions of orbital debris.

About This Project

Project Background

OrbitalMind is a machine learning application developed to address one of aerospace's most pressing challenges — the growing density of space debris in Sun-Synchronous Orbits. With over 14,000 tracked objects and millions of untracked fragments, the orbital environment demands intelligent, data-driven risk assessment tools.

The project uses DBSCAN (Density-Based Spatial Clustering of Applications with Noise) — an algorithm uniquely suited for this domain. Unlike k-means, DBSCAN requires no prior assumption of cluster count, handles irregular debris cloud shapes naturally, and marks sparse outliers as noise rather than forcing them into clusters. These properties make it ideal for the chaotic geometry of orbital debris distributions.

Data is sourced from Space-Track.org — the US Space Force's official catalog — providing TLE (Two-Line Element) sets updated multiple times daily. The pipeline extracts six orbital elements per object: inclination, RAAN, argument of perigee, eccentricity, mean motion, and epoch. Objects in the 800–1000 km altitude band are filtered for ISRO-specific analysis.

Visualization uses Plotly's 3D scatter globe to render all debris objects in true orbital geometry, with cluster assignments color-coded and object classes (debris, rocket bodies, payloads) distinguished visually. ISRO operational satellites are overlaid with real-time conjunction proximity indicators.

This project demonstrates how classical ML algorithms remain highly effective for geospatial scientific problems when combined with domain-appropriate feature engineering and interactive visualization — no deep learning required.
